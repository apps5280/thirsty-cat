Support

Thirsty Cat is a wave-defense arcade game where a cow defends her farm from milk-thirsty alien invaders. If something isn't working the way you'd expect — or you just want to ask a question — this page has the most common answers and a contact link below.

Common questions

How do I shoot?

Touch and pull on the cow's udder to aim. Release to fire a milk arc. The pull distance controls power, and the angle controls the arc. The longer you've played, the more your fingers will know where to land it without thinking.

Enemies are coming too fast.

Thirsty Cat's difficulty ramps with each wave by design — you're meant to lose eventually. If you're stuck on a particular wave, try chaining shots without releasing your touch in between (each release fires one shot), and prioritize the enemies closest to your cow over the ones farther away.

Game Center says I'm not signed in.

Open Settings → Game Center in iOS (not in the game) and sign in to your Apple ID's Game Center. Thirsty Cat uses Game Center for leaderboards and achievements — neither is required to play, but if you want your scores to count online, you need to be signed in.

My progress disappeared after I reinstalled.

Thirsty Cat saves your progress locally on your device. Deleting and reinstalling the app — or switching to a new phone without a full backup — clears your high score and any unlocked content. Game Center leaderboard scores survive because Apple holds those.

The audio is delayed or muted.

Other apps (music, podcasts, calls) can interrupt the game's audio session. Try closing those and relaunching Thirsty Cat. If you're using Bluetooth speakers or headphones, expect slightly higher latency — wired or on-device speakers will feel more responsive.

I have an idea or a bug report.

Email it. Bug reports especially are welcome — include your iPhone model and iOS version if you can. Reproducible steps help most. Screenshots or a screen recording are gold.
